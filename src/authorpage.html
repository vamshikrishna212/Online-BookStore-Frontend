Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Biography:       Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was an esteemed
            aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu,
            Kalam rose from humble beginnings to become a key figure in India's space and missile programs. His work at
            the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)
            was instrumental in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and its strategic missile
            systems. 

                  As President from 2002 to 2007, Kalam was beloved for his simplicity, humility, and vision
            for India's future. He championed education, innovation, and self-reliance, often engaging directly with students and
            youth, whom he inspired to dream big and work towards the nation's development. His book *"Wings of Fire,"*
            an autobiography, is widely read and reflects his journey and ideals.
            
                  Even after his presidency, Kalam continued to work tirelessly as a teacher and mentor until
            his passing on July 27, 2015. He remains a symbol of perseverance, vision, and dedication to the service of the nation,
            fondly remembered as the "People's President."
